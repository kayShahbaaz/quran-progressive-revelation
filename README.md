# The Qur'ān's Progressive Revelation: Al-ʿAlaq to Al-Fātiḥah

A study tracing the first five Surahs of the Qur'ān in the order they were revealed — Al-ʿAlaq, Al-Qalam, Al-Muzzammil, Al-Muddaththir, and Al-Fātiḥah. Each Surah is read against the Seerah context of the Beloved Prophet's ﷺ life at the moment it descended, alongside an āyah-by-āyah look at its Arabic roots, structure, and meaning.

## Overview

Revelation didn't arrive as a finished book. It arrived in pieces, into a real life, at real moments — a man alone in a cave, a man wrapped in his cloak and exhausted, a man commanded to rise and warn a hostile city, and finally a community taught how to pray. Reading these five Surahs in the order they came down shows a clear arc: from the command to read, to the formation of character, to the discipline of the night, to the public mission, to the prayer that holds all of it together.
